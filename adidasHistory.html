The History of Addidas

1920s:

Adi Dassler, a cobbler from Herzogenaurach, Germany, began making sports shoes in his mother's washroom after World War I.

1924:

The Dassler brothers officially registered their company, Gebr√ºder Dassler Schuhfabrik (Geda), which means "Dassler Brothers Shoe Factory".

1936:

Adi Dassler convinced Jesse Owens to wear his spiked running shoes at the Berlin Olympics, where Owens won four gold medals. This event brought Adidas to the attention of the world.

1940s:

World War II disrupted Adidas, and the Dassler brothers worked to rebuild the company. However, a rift between the brothers grew, and in 1948, Rudolf left the company to start his own, which became Puma. Adi rebranded his company as "Adi Dassler Adidas Sportschuhfabrik".

1950s:

Adidas grew as football players adopted the company's lightweight shoes.

1960s:

Adidas introduced soccer balls in 1963 and expanded into apparel in 1967. The company also developed the first specialist running shoe and the Azteca Gold sprint spike for the Mexico Olympics.

1972:

Adidas introduced the Trefoil logo, which symbolizes performance.

2020:

The Adidas Originals Superstar shoe celebrated its 50th anniversary.
