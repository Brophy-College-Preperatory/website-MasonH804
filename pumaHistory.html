The History of Puma

Founding

In 1924, Rudolf and Adolf "Adi" Dassler founded the Dassler Brothers Shoe Factory in Herzogenaurach, Germany.

Split

In 1948, the brothers split and Rudolf founded Puma, originally named Ruda after himself. Adolf founded Adidas, named after himself.

First major success

In 1952, Puma released the Super Atom, the first football boot with screw-in studs.

Logo

In 1967, German cartoonist Lutz Backes created Puma's famous jumping cat logo. In 1968, the company added a white form strip to the logo and shoes.

Olympic success

In the 1960s, PUMA-wearing athletes won gold medals and broke world records at the Rome and Tokyo Olympics.

Athlete endorsements

In the 1970s, 1980s, and 1990s, Puma signed deals with some of the biggest names in football, basketball, and tennis.

Modern era

Puma continues to work with top athletes, including Usain Bolt, Sergio Agüero, Lewis Hamilton, and Neymar.
